Pokémon is a Japanese media franchise that spans video games, animated television series and films, manga, a trading card game, merchandise, and various other forms of media. The franchise is set in a shared fictional universe where humans live alongside creatures called Pokémon. These creatures exist in hundreds of distinct species, each possessing unique abilities, elemental types, and evolutionary forms. Humans, known as Pokémon Trainers, catch, train, and battle Pokémon while also forming strong bonds with them. The franchise primarily targets children aged 5 to 12, but its appeal extends to teenagers and adults worldwide. Pokémon has become the highest-grossing media franchise of all time, surpassing other major entertainment properties, and remains one of the most influential and best-selling video game franchises in history.

Pokémon originated as a pair of role-playing video games developed by Game Freak, based on an idea by its founder, Satoshi Tajiri. Inspired by Tajiri’s childhood interest in collecting insects, the concept focused on exploration, collection, and social interaction. The first games were released for the Nintendo Game Boy on February 27, 1996, in Japan. Despite modest expectations, they became sleeper hits and gained popularity through word of mouth.

Following the success of the games, Pokémon expanded rapidly into other media. A manga adaptation, a trading card game, and an animated television series were introduced, further broadening its audience. By the late 1990s, Pokémon had been exported internationally, triggering a global cultural phenomenon often referred to as “Pokémania.” During this period, Pokémon became deeply embedded in popular culture, particularly among children.

Although the peak of Pokémania declined by the early 2000s, the franchise did not fade. Instead, Pokémon evolved into a long-running and consistently successful brand, with new video game generations, animated series, films, and merchandise released regularly. Each new generation introduces new regions, Pokémon species, gameplay mechanics, and storylines. In 2016, Pokémon experienced a renewed surge in global popularity with the release of Pokémon Go, an augmented reality mobile game developed by Niantic. The game encouraged real-world exploration and social interaction, attracting millions of players and introducing Pokémon to a new generation of fans. Today, Pokémon continues to be a major force in entertainment, maintaining its relevance through innovation, nostalgia, and its enduring themes of friendship, adventure, and discovery.
